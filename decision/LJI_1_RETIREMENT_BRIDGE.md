# Bridge note retiring LJI 1.0

The v1 Ledger Justification Index is retired as a current decision rule. It may be described only as an archived simulation-stage heuristic.

## Retired elements

- The scalar `benefit - cost` form is not sufficient as an adoption rule because it did not state axioms, independence conditions or hard constraints [ASSUMED:v2.0.0-protocol].
- The v1 thresholds `-0.05` and `+0.10` are retired because no derivation, expert validation or legal/proportionality semantics were supplied [ASSUMED:v2.0.0-protocol].
- Scenario labels such as “borderline”, “A3 may be justified” or “A1/A2 preferred” are retired as current conclusions when derived from v1 LJI thresholds [ASSUMED:v2.0.0-protocol].

## Surviving material

The v1 factors are not discarded completely. Dispute risk, organisational multiplicity, revocation pressure, operational burden and signature-profile pressure survive only as candidate variables within the LJI 2.0 criteria register. They no longer decide backend selection unless the v2.0.0 axioms, v2.0.0 legal mappings, external-service measurements and Delphi calibration support them.

## Manuscript rule

Any reconstructed manuscript sentence that mentions v1 LJI must use past-tense historical language and must not cite v1 LJI as an adoption result. Current recommendations must be phrased as pending or parametric until validation is complete.
