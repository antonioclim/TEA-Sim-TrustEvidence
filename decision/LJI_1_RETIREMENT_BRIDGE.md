# Bridge note retiring LJI 1.0

The v1 Ledger Justification Index is retired as a current decision rule. It may be described only as an archived simulation-stage heuristic.

## Retired elements

- The scalar `benefit - cost` form is not sufficient as an adoption rule because it did not state axioms, independence conditions or hard constraints.
- The v1 thresholds `-0.05` and `+0.10` are retired because no derivation, expert validation or legal/proportionality semantics were supplied.
- Scenario labels such as “borderline”, “A3 may be justified” or “A1/A2 preferred” are retired as current conclusions when derived from v1 LJI thresholds.

## Surviving material

The v1 factors are not discarded completely. Dispute risk, organisational multiplicity, revocation pressure, operational burden and signature-profile pressure survive only as candidate variables within the LJI 2.0 criteria register. They no longer decide backend selection unless the decision layer axioms, legal traceability legal mappings, external benchmark run measurements and Delphi calibration support them.

## Manuscript rule

Any v2.0.0 manuscript sentence that mentions v1 LJI must use past-tense historical language and must not cite v1 LJI as an adoption result. Current recommendations must be phrased as requires external validation or parametric until validation is complete.
