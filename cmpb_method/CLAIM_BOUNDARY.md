# Claim boundary

## Supported by executed evidence

- closed structural and semantic validation for the supplied synthetic event classes;
- rejection of the tested payload-leakage and direct-identifier controls;
- deterministic canonicalisation under the TE-JCS-1 admission profile;
- local Ed25519 signature and receipt verification with explicit fixture key registries;
- index/tree-size-bound local inclusion verification;
- retained-checkpoint consistency verification for the tested larger-tree advances;
- controlled mutation rejection;
- deterministic property-based tests and finite bounded executable checks;
- bounded single-host synthetic workload passage;
- deterministic figure regeneration.

## Not supported

- clinical validation or clinical utility;
- production deployment, scalability or service-level performance;
- confidentiality, anonymisation or source truth;
- global non-equivocation, witness gossip or split-view detection between isolated verifiers;
- security following compromise of an authorised signing key;
- operational key lifecycle, revocation or hardware-backed custody;
- occurrence uniqueness for duplicate evidence material;
- FHIR, BALP, IHE, GDPR, EHDS or ISO conformance/compliance;
- formal proof, theorem or complete verification.
