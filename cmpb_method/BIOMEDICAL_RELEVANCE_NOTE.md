# Biomedical relevance of the synthetic fixtures

The method treats audit evidence as a biomedical data-curation object rather than as an incidental application log. The fixtures describe accountability events produced while synthetic personal-monitoring objects are registered, accessed, consented, transformed, disclosed, aggregated or rejected.

The retained evidence can express pseudonymous subject context, the operational actor, the software emitter, purpose, policy and consent bindings, object versions, provenance relations, aggregation counts and signed local receipt material. Raw monitoring samples, direct identifiers, credentials and payload-commitment nonces are excluded from the public envelope.

The examples establish a bounded biomedical information-processing use case. They do not establish clinical utility, patient benefit, diagnostic validity, production deployment or population representativeness.
